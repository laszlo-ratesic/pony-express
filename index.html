Postcards Through Hell

Tales of the blood-soaked messengers delivering U.S. mail in Afghanistan

About the Book

A team of private security contractors is recruited to Afghanistan, working high-risk contracts for SOC (Special Operations Consulting). As SOC expands their operations into the region, their assignments are wrought with less-than-optimal conditions and inadequate supplies as they try to gain a foothold in the surging market for private military contracts.

Battling Taliban Militia, local drug warlords, and their own personal demons, accrued from years as soldiers and soldiers-for-hire. These men called The Pony Express, will tell all. The book depicts the rarely seen side of the war effort in the Middle East, exploring why these operations are often hidden from the public and examining the psychological impact on these security contractors who risk their lives to deliver the mail.

The Authors

Edward Ford

Edward Ford retired from the United States Marine Corps as an infantry and Force Reconnaissance Marine in 2004. He served in many countries around the world as both a Marine and a Tier 1 Security Contractor, including combat service during the First Gulf War, Somalia, Iraq, and Afghanistan.

Alan Chiasson

Alan Chiasson served in the United States Navy as a Hospital Corpsman. He is currently certified as a Paramedic in the state of Texas and has several deployments overseas, in both Iraq and Afghanistan providing high-risk medical support for PSD, convoy, and force protection operations.
